Define Your WHO

Your WHO is your internal identity made up of your Values, Pillars and Ideal Emotion. It appears when you are operating at your best self. (Your external identity are your physical attributes, achievements, job title, family titles [someone's child, spouse, sibling, parent]).

Conflict is created by believing your internal identity was attacked. When you identify why you are upset, you can de-escalate, then operate as your best self to manage challenges.

This guided WHO Assessment helps you identify:

  • Values — your non-negotiables and how you think the world should act. Values are the core essence of who you are. They are the fuel that propels you to act. When you are living in alignment with your Values, they are what helps you to achieve your goals.
  • Pillars — are characteristics that describe who you are as your happiest, most relaxed self.
  • Ideal Emotion — what you want to feel every day.
  • Trigger — the demoralizing inner critic telling you "I'm not .... enough".

How it works

    Practical Application: Clarity comes by identifying conflicting Values.
  2. Pillars

    Practical Application: Wear your Pillars as jackets to frame your mindset or frame a hard conversation.

    4. Road Test: Living your Values and being your Pillars allows you to feel your Ideal Emotion.
  4. Trigger
    Practical Application: When your demoralizing Trigger gets activated, pivot to your Pillars and use those words as your response team.
